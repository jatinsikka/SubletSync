FAQ

Questions we get every day.

Is SubletSync actually safe? How is it different from Craigslist?

Craigslist and Facebook have zero identity verification — anyone can post anything. SubletSync requires every user to verify their .edu email, LinkedIn, or government ID before listing or messaging. Every deposit is held in licensed escrow, and every agreement is a legally binding document. We also run AI scam detection on every message in real time.

What exactly happens to my deposit in escrow?

When you agree to a sublease, the first month's deposit is transferred to our FDIC-insured escrow partner — not to the lister. It's held there until your confirmed move-in date. If the listing falls through on the lister's end, you get a full automatic refund. If you back out, the refund policy follows what's in your signed sublease agreement.

Can non-students use SubletSync?

Yes. Anyone can create an account with any email. .edu users receive a verified badge and higher trust score by default, but young professionals, recent grads, and anyone else searching in a college town can use the platform fully. We're built for the college-adjacent market — not exclusively for current students.

What data do you collect and do you sell it?

We collect your email, name, university, and any info you choose to put on your profile. We use this only to power the platform — verification, matching, and communication. We never sell your data to advertisers or third parties. You can request full data deletion at any time from your account settings.

What if something goes wrong with my sublease?

Because every SubletSync sublease comes with a signed digital agreement, you have legal recourse that Craigslist or GroupMe arrangements don't provide. Our support team is available 7 days a week, and in cases of confirmed fraud by a verified user, we maintain a dispute resolution fund to make affected parties whole.

How does the lifestyle compatibility score work?

During sign-up, you complete a short questionnaire on sleep schedule, cleanliness standards, guest preferences, noise tolerance, and pet policies. When you browse listings, each one gets a compatibility percentage based on how closely your answers match the lister's. It's entirely optional, but users who use it report significantly better roommate experiences.
